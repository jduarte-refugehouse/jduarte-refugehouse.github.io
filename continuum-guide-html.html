Welcome to The Continuum

The Continuum represents a revolutionary way of understanding and supporting individual experiences within our agency. Instead of seeing separate events, documents, and tasks, we see the complete, connected story of each person's journey.

Think of it this way:

Old Way: Like looking at separate photos in a box - disconnected snapshots

Continuum Way: Like watching a movie - everything flows together into one story

Why This Matters

Every child, foster parent, and staff member has their own continuum - their complete journey through our system. These individual stories don't exist in isolation; they overlap, intersect, and influence each other in profound ways.

The Core Concept

The Continuum connects all the pieces of someone's experience so we can see the whole picture. It recognizes that:

Past Shapes Present

Previous experiences, placements, and patterns continue to influence today's behaviors and needs

Present Creates Future

Today's interventions, relationships, and decisions determine tomorrow's outcomes

Everything Connects

Documents trigger tasks, tasks create outcomes, outcomes influence future requirements

Key Innovation: Document Frame Gallery

Like an art gallery with empty frames waiting for paintings, Document Frames show what documentation is needed before it arrives:

The Gallery Concept

  • Empty frames are visible reminders - You see what's missing at a glance
  • Frames define requirements - Each frame knows what document fits
  • Progress is visual - "7 of 12 frames filled"
  • Frames can be smart - Urgent ones become prominent, overdue ones alert
  • Workflows create galleries - A new placement might create 15 empty frames at once

Key Innovation: Things Exist Before They Happen

In the Continuum, elements exist in multiple states:

  • Required: A document exists as a requirement before it's created
  • Scheduled: An appointment exists as a plan before it occurs
  • Active: Tasks are in progress
  • Complete: Finished with documented outcomes
  • Historical: Past events still influencing the present

The Ripple Effect

When something happens in one continuum, it creates ripples that spread to others:

Example Ripple:

Child has a good day at school → Foster parent receives positive feedback → Foster parent feels encouraged → Home atmosphere improves → Child sleeps better → Child has another good day
