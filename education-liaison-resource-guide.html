üéØ TBRI Quick Reference for Schools

Connecting Principles

  • Be Present: Attend school events, meet teachers regularly
  • Eye Contact: Help teachers understand why some kids avoid it
  • Voice Quality: Model calm, playful engagement
  • Safe Touch: Advocate for appropriate physical boundaries

Empowering Principles

  • Transitions: Request visual schedules, warnings before changes
  • Sensory Needs: Advocate for movement breaks, fidgets, quiet spaces
  • Choices: Help teachers offer controlled choices
  • Predictability: Consistent routines and expectations

Correcting Principles

  • Proactive Strategies: Prevention over punishment
  • Re-dos: Practice correct behavior immediately
  • Natural Consequences: Logical, not punitive
  • Time-In: Connection before correction

üö® Crisis Response Protocols

Educational Crisis Definition: Any situation that threatens the child's school placement, safety, or academic progress requiring immediate intervention.

Crisis Response Flowchart

Step 1: Assess Severity
  • Immediate safety concern? ‚Üí Call 911 first
  • Suspension/Expulsion threat? ‚Üí Immediate response needed
  • Academic failure? ‚Üí Urgent but not immediate
Step 2: Notify Required Parties
  • Supervisor immediately
  • SSCC/DFPS within 3 business days
  • Foster parents ASAP
  • Treatment team as appropriate
Step 3: Intervene Using TBRI
  • De-escalate with calm presence
  • Advocate for alternative to suspension
  • Request immediate meeting
  • Bring support team if needed
Step 4: Document Everything
  • Timeline of events
  • All communications
  • Interventions attempted
  • Outcomes and next steps

Common Crisis Scenarios

Suspension/Expulsion Threat

Immediate Actions:

  • Request manifestation determination
  • Advocate for in-school alternatives
  • Bring behavioral support plan
  • Document all meetings

School Refusal

TBRI Response:

  • Identify fear/trigger
  • Create felt safety plan
  • Gradual re-entry if needed
  • Coordinate with therapist

Bullying Incident

Advocacy Steps:

  • Document all incidents
  • Request safety plan
  • Involve administration
  • Consider classroom change
